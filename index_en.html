Teaching assistant (ATER) at UPMF and LIG


I am currently postdoc at the LIMSI laboratory (Paris).


My actual research focus on the information extraction in multi-modal data, image/audio/video processing (video OCR, face and speaker recognition), multi-modal fusion (classifier based, constraint clustering, propagation intra and cross modal), on the unsupervised/semi-supervised annotation and active learning process.

Research activities

Camomile framework: For the camomile project, we developed a prototype of a collaborative annotation framework of multi-modal, multimedia and multi-lingual data on multiple site. This framework can be used via web interface for manual annotations or via automatique systems. It will be tested during a new task at mediaeval 2015 (source code on github)

Organisation of the new task "Multimodal Person Discovery in Broadcast TV" at MediaEval 2015 (benchmarking initiative dedicated to evaluating new algorithms for multimedia access and retrieval). In this task, we proposed to the community to find person present in broadcast news TV without the used of biometric models trained a priori but in using informations available in the video (pronounced or written names). For this campaign, we provided to participant an important set of mono-modal and multi-modal systems (source code on github)

Optical character recognition in videos (Video OCR): we developed LOOV a video OCR tool (source code on github) that, first, find spatial-temporal positions of overlaid texts on screen and then adapt these images to a standard OCR software. A combination of several transcripts of the same text box allows to increase the quality of transcription. This tool has been used to extract person names written in a title box in broadcast TV. This source of information is importante for person identification in this type of media.

Multimodal person identification in broadcast TV: with biometric models trained a priori or by the propagation of overlaid names onto speakers/faces clusters.

Extracting person names from web press to build little worlds related to news: The web press is an interesting source to know who made ​​the news during a time period and links between people making this news. The extraction of named entities from the multitude of newspaper articles published on the web every day helped us to create little worlds of people who make the news. These little worlds may, for example, propose a short list of people who can appear in other media such as TV broadcast.

Spatio-temporal analysis of dynamic MRI to detect suspicious areas of the prostate cancer: After injection of a contrast medium, the MRI sequences acquisition of the prostate can highlight areas of potentially cancer. These areas change appearance differently than healthy areas. The use of the temporal correlation coefficient allowed us to validate the feasibility of a technique based on the comparison of the contrast agent concentration evolution in the patients tissue.

Projects

Quaero

Collaborative program of innovation and industrial research on automatic analysis and enrichment of digital, multimedia and multilingual content.

Qcompere

Quaero consortium for Multimodal Persons Recognition is a group of laboratory and company founded for the REPERE challenge. This challenge is an evaluation project in the field of person recognition in TV broadcast. This project tries to answer four questions: Who is seen ? Who is speaking ? Whose name is written on screen ? Whose name is pronounced ? These questions can been answered in a monomodal way (use of a single modality to answer the question associated) or in a multimodal way (using only videos of the corpus or all available resources : training corpus, web data, etc).

Camomille

Collaborative Annotation Of Multi-MOdal, MultI-Lingual And Multi-MEdia Documents is a project that aims to develop a first prototype of collaborative annotation data on 3M (multimodal, multimedia and multilingual), wherein the manual annotation will be done remotely on many sites with automatic pre-annotations (iterative learning techniques, data selection for active learning strategies, ...).
